The Problem

The problem is no longer theoretical

In November 2025, drones halted flight operations over Volkel and Eindhoven air bases. The Netherlands committed between 1 and 2.5 billion euros to accelerated counter-drone capability. The new Defensienota makes counter-drone a core pillar of the Dutch armed forces, with a dedicated development lab for short-cycle innovation with Dutch industry. At the European level, the drone wall initiative (EDDI) targets initial operational capability by the end of 2026, and the February 2026 EU Action Plan on Drone and Counter-Drone Security names real-time detection capacity as the priority.

The market is answering with sensors and effectors: radars, RF detectors, jammers, interceptors. What remains thin is the layer above the sensors. Given thousands of simultaneous tracks from many sensors, which ones are hostile behaviour, and can the decision be defended afterwards? That is not a sensor problem. It is a pattern-detection-with-evidence problem.

Detection

What VIGIL detects

Five hostile-behaviour typologies, separated from the legitimate traffic they hide in. Each is adjudicated deterministically, with full decision provenance.

01

Loiter surveillance

Sustained dwell near critical assets and restricted zones, separated from legitimate transit.

02

Corridor incursion

Presence in prohibited airspace, adjudicated deny-class with full decision provenance.

03

Coordinated swarms

Correlated kinematics across multiple tracks: bounded separation, matched headings, connected components of coordinated behaviour.

04

Perimeter probing

Repeated boundary approaches without entry: the reconnaissance pattern that precedes an incursion.

05

Identity spoofing

Remote ID identities whose reported motion is physically impossible: one identity, two places.

Federation

Detect jointly. Share nothing.

Civil air traffic control, military radar, and private site sensors cannot legally pool raw surveillance data. VIGIL's federation layer, built on multi-party computation, lets independent sensor operators detect coordinated hostile behaviour that none of them can see alone, without any raw track leaving its owner. The same cryptographic federation already runs across financial institutions in the ZQUAS financial crime tenant.

Evidence

Evidence-grade by construction

Every VIGIL decision is deterministic and replayable. Every detection carries its provenance: which signals, from which sensors, through which policy, under which validated detection floor. Detection quality is enforced against independently generated hostile scenarios with rise-only performance floors, the same validation discipline the engine's financial crime tenant maintains under FCA Digital Sandbox scrutiny. When an interception decision is questioned, the answer is a replay, not a reconstruction.
