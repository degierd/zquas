The visualization maps a compliance monitoring network larger than Amsterdam. Each building represents an entity cluster (a bank branch, a corporate group, a correspondent chain). Each illuminated window represents an individual account. Window color reflects real-time risk classification: safe (blue/cyan), review (yellow), elevated (orange), high (red), critical (red pulse). Risk scores propagate through the entity graph via GPU-accelerated GNN inference. The drill-down panel shows individual account details including financial activity, risk flags, alert status, and neural lattice ML scoring.

This is the actual ZQUAS engine running on a single NVIDIA RTX 5090. Not a pre-rendered animation. Not a mockup. The transaction stream, risk evaluation, alert generation, and visualization all execute on GPU in real time.

Note: Transaction throughput varies during the recording as the engine processes different phases. Peak sustained TPS of 3,530,000 was recorded during this session. The benchmark figure of 2.58M GDPS (governance decisions per second) cited elsewhere on this site reflects the governance benchmark suite which measures policy adjudication throughput specifically — each decision evaluating all 29 policies simultaneously. This is a different and more conservative measurement than raw transaction ingestion TPS.
